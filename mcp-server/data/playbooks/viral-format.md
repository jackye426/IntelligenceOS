Look for moments where the expert gives a specific patient action around a high-stakes situation, such as what to ask before surgery, why a scan or laparoscopy may miss endo, what symptoms are not normal, or what to say to finally be taken seriously.

## Hook & packaging rules (from approved A/B learnings)

- Prefer imperative patient-action hooks ("always ask for photos", "ask for excision") over soft interview or Q&A-reply openings on surgical/advocacy content.
- For scan content, stress who interprets the scan, not just that you had a scan.
- For parent/teen pain content, use a parent-education frame rather than a truncated symptom-only opener.
- When reposting proven clips, preserve the original winning hook structure — repackaging often underperforms even when the script is identical.

## Anti-patterns

- Soft interview open without a patient action in the first seconds.
- Service-only CTA cold open without a clinical teaching moment.
- Copying a single viral outlier hook without matching playbook theme.
- Treating same-topic Q&A reply clips as equivalent to imperative surgical CTAs.
